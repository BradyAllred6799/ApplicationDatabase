# Lesson 1 of 7 — Introduction and Overview

## Welcome to Asset Management

This course is designed for University of Florida employees responsible for managing and tracking university property, and to provide all UF employees with basic knowledge of how to identify and responsibly manage assets at the University of Florida. # Lesson 2 of 7 — UF Assets and Their Identifying Decals

## Objectives

1. Define what constitutes a UF asset and describe its life cycle.
2. Identify the different decals UF uses to identify and track department assets and required non-capital assets.
3. Explain the importance of identifying and tracking property.

> **Assets are property owned by the University of Florida or for which the University of Florida has contractual responsibility.**

Tracking these assets is crucial for financial accountability, compliance with state and federal regulations, and maximizing resource use. To accomplish this, the university uses a system of color-coded decals to identify each type of property.

## What Are the Different Kinds of Assets?

### Capital Assets (Yellow or Orange Decal)

These assets are included on the university's financial statements and are verified by an annual physical inventory. Examples include furniture, scientific equipment, art, vehicles, and livestock. The university uses two different decals for capital assets to distinguish between new and legacy items:

- **Orange Decal:** Identifies "legacy" assets. These are items that were designated as capital property under previous rules (before July 1, 2025) when the cost threshold may have been lower. They will remain capital assets for their entire life, even if their original cost is less than the current $10,000 threshold.
- **Yellow Decal:** Identifies all new capital assets that are acquired and cost $10,000 or more.

For capital assets, Asset Management creates the property record, which generates the decal number.

### Required Non-Capital Assets (White Decal)

Based on the Florida Administrative Code (691-72.002), certain items costing less than $10,000 must be tracked. These are identified with a white decal. This mandatory category includes:

- **Hard Drives:** Anything with a hard drive, including laptops, desktops, tablets, smartphones, VR Headsets, printers, and copiers. (This does not apply to printers or copiers leased through the PrintSmart program.)
- **Registered Transport:** All vehicles, vessels, ATVs, golf carts, LSVs, trailers, aircraft, and drones that are titled, licensed, or registered with the Department of Motor Vehicles or the Federal Aviation Administration.
- **ITAR Items:** Items on the United States Munitions List (USML) or in the 600 series Commerce Control List, which have military applications (e.g., firearms, night vision goggles, Geo-synchronous mapping).
- **Fund 163:** Any asset acquired on fund code 163 Auxiliary-Equipment Use Fee Fund.

For required non-capital assets, the department creates the property record in MyAssets and then forwards the decal number to Asset Management.

### Agency Property (Green Decal)

Agency property is a special kind of asset that UF acquires through a sponsored funding source, as a loan from a federal or non-federal agency, or as a donation. The various entities, including the federal government, require this property to be tracked, so it will have its primary UF Decal (white, yellow, or orange) AND a green decal. Depending on the agreement, UF may be required to return the property to the sponsoring agency.

### Optional Departmental Tracking (Blue Decal)

A department may choose to track items for its own inventory purposes even if not required by regulations. These optionally tracked assets are marked with a blue decal.

## University Vehicles

University Vehicles—passenger cars, trucks, trailers, vessels, and aircraft—are treated like any other UF capital asset: they must be tracked from acquisition through disposal and are managed under the same life-cycle procedures that govern all equipment and required non-capital assets. Facilities Services updates each vehicle in the vehicle intake form and assigns a unique vehicle number (VH#). Visit [Obtaining or Transferring a License Plate or Title for a University Vehicle | CFO Division](https://cfo.ufl.edu/procedures-training-resources/inventory-and-property-management/obtaining-or-transferring-a-license-plate-or-title-for-a-university-vehicle-procedures/) to review the procedure for obtaining a license plate or title.

Asset Management is the only department authorized to obtain license plates, titles, or registrations for all UF vehicles, vessels, trailers, or aircraft from the Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) and Federal Aviation Administration (FAA). If your department is in possession of the title to a vehicle or vessel, please immediately contact Asset Management at (352) 392-2556.

# Lesson 3 of 7 — Personnel and Responsibilities

## Objectives

1. Summarize the responsibilities different individuals/groups have for managing and tracking property.
2. Explain the importance of identifying and tracking property.
3. Determine what surplus property is, locate and use the UF Surplus Warehouse, and describe how users are authorized.
4. List resources available to assist with property management.

> **Utilization, Securing, and Safeguarding UF Assets is Everyone's Responsibility.**

Tracking and identifying property:

- Minimizes risks for the university and facilitates compliance with state and sponsor guidelines
- Maximizes use of property
- Helps in sustainability efforts

## Property Custodians and Department Contacts

Each department is responsible for decaling, identifying, annual inventorying, transferring, and disposing of all assets in their departments.

- **Property Custodian:** The Property Custodian is responsible for all assets and approves all transactions. Based on specific departments, the property custodian's responsibility is delegated to the Vice Presidents, Deans, etc.
- **Departmental Contact:** The Departmental Contact handles day-to-day operations related to assets and is responsible for completing the required asset inventory. They may assign staff as SCANNERS who can assist them with the process.

## Understanding Individual Responsibilities

Managing UF property requires all individuals to safeguard assets and ensure proper use. Responsibilities include completing off-site certifications and following protocols for foreign travel with UF-owned items. Asset Management has created the [myAssets Property Management System](https://cfo.ufl.edu/procedures-training-resources/inventory-and-property-management/myassets-property-management-system/), which enables individuals responsible for the property to have up-to-date information to manage the property and submit forms electronically for final approval at Asset Management.

## Responsibilities for All UF Employees

### Completing Off-Site Certifications

Off-site certifications are required for all assets which are temporarily or permanently located at non-UF facilities. For example, an off-site certification is not required if a laptop travels from an office on campus to a home office work location. If the item is used at a UF facility, it is not off-site. Temporarily means that the asset is not in a UF location for more than a month. Annually, individuals are notified electronically, via email, with a request to complete the online form.

### Foreign Travel With UF Property/Equipment

Because U.S. export laws occasionally will require an export license to take property to a specific country, UF policy requires that a Foreign Travel Request must be submitted before any UF-owned property can be taken outside the United States. Contact Asset Management with any questions about [taking UF property outside the United States](https://training.hr.ufl.edu/instructionguides/asset_management/complete_foreign_travel_request.pdf).

### Safeguarding and Care

To minimize loss or theft of property, control your room, space, and equipment.

### Cost-Saving

Check [UF Surplus](https://surplus.ufl.edu/) before purchasing new items. UF Surplus contains many items that are available at no cost to departments.

### Communicating Timely With Your Department's Contact(s)

Communicate timely with your department's contact(s) when:

- Equipment moves to a new location
- Equipment is leaving campus
- Equipment is broken/obsolete/damaged/inoperable
- Equipment is missing/lost/stolen/improperly disposed (improperly thrown away)
- Equipment is no longer needed and needs to be sent to UF Surplus
- Equipment is manufactured at UF and costs over $10,000 in total
- New equipment is donated or loaned (furnished) to UF
- UF receives new property donated through the UF Foundation, transferred in with an incoming faculty, or loaned through a contract or grant
- Faculty or staff members responsible for equipment leave the university

> **UF assets should NEVER be given away, thrown away, taken, or sold without authorization from Asset Management.**

### Primary Investigators Leaving the University of Florida with Equipment

Equipment may be donated to another university when a primary investigator leaves the University of Florida under specific conditions:

- Equipment purchased on an active project is transferred with the PI.
- Equipment purchased with federal dollars that is no longer needed at UF may transfer to the PI's new research institution if needed for the PI's ongoing research.
- Equipment a PI brings with them from another institution (donated to UF) is transferred to the new institution when a PI leaves.

All donations must be approved by Asset Management.

## Role-Specific Responsibilities

- **Fiscal Staff:** Process purchase orders for equipment or required non-capital assets.
- **Department Staff:** Manage off-site certification (mobile devices, laptops); receive equipment; work with Asset Management to tag/inventory.
- **Chairs, Directors, and Department Heads:** Safeguard & care of assets in the department; ensure property maintenance; monitor utilization.
- **Asset Management:** Maintain the university's official property records for all capital assets, including recording and decaling new assets and updating property records.

# Lesson 4 of 7 — The Life of an Asset

## Objectives

1. Define what constitutes a UF asset and describe its life cycle.
2. Explain the importance of identifying and tracking property.
3. Outline procedures for managing and tracking property.
4. Determine what surplus property is, locate and use the UF Surplus Warehouse, and describe how users are authorized.

## Phase One: Asset Acquisition

There are several ways to acquire property for UF:

- **myUFmarketplace:** Items can be purchased via the online portal, which is the most common method of acquisition; equipment may be bought from vendors or, for larger items, fabricated in-house by assembling smaller pieces.
- **UF Property Swap Listserv:** Asset Management facilitates property exchange between UF departments and is open to faculty and staff for UF purposes only; it includes descriptions, pictures, and locations of items, and departments arrange transfers independently.
- **UF Surplus:** Surplus property is available for transfer from UF Surplus at no cost to the department; only users pre-authorized by their Property Custodian may pick up surplus property.
- **Donations:** Assets can be donated through the UF Foundation or transferred by incoming faculty; these donations and transfers can include diverse items such as livestock, vehicles, and equipment.
- **Government Furnished - Loaned:** Assets loaned by agencies for University use remain the property of the agency, while UF is responsible for those assets and includes them in its inventory.

### Assets Acquired Through Procurement

A requisition for an asset is entered into myUFmarketplace and undergoes requisition and purchase order approval workflows. Asset Management is involved in the purchase order workflow before dispatch.

Purchase Orders flow to Asset Management under these conditions:

- If the account code is 78XXXX.
- If the commodity code and cost per line is $10,000 or more.
- If certain non-78XXXX account codes have a cost per line $10,000 or more (e.g., lab supplies), Asset Management reviews these to ensure the correct account code is used.

## Phase Two: Decal Creation and Placement

Asset Management reviews and approves invoices for equipment and prints decals (property tags) to track the property. Asset Management prints capital decals based on invoice payment.

Some departments have Self-Scanning Department (SSD) status, allowing them to handle their own inventory. In these cases, capital decals are mailed to the department for placement. Regardless of status, if the item is a required asset that is not capital (white decal), the Asset Management office will mail the decals to the department.

## Phase Three: Inventory

Once an asset is decaled or tagged (yellow, orange, or white), it becomes part of the annual capital inventory schedule.

As required by the Board of Governors, an annual inventory of all capital assets is conducted to ensure proper accounting. This process takes nearly a year to complete as UF inventories over 80,000 assets across campus and off-site locations. The annual inventory is performed from **July 1st** (beginning of the fiscal year) to **March 1st**.

Asset Management has developed the **myInventory app**, which assists in the inventory process. This app can be downloaded on various Apple and Android devices, allowing users to scan their own inventory. Departments are responsible for locating missing items.

## Phase 4: Disposal

When an asset has served its useful life and is no longer needed, it is ready to undergo the disposal process.

> In support of the UF Strategic Plan and the university's sustainability initiative, no university property may be thrown away without prior approval from Asset Management and should be sent to UF Surplus.

### Reusing and Refurbishing Assets

At UF Surplus, property is reused, refurbished, and recycled to maximize its value. The Surplus PC shop refurbishes equipment to meet UFIT standards, allowing departments to request these items at no cost.

### Disposal Procedures and Restrictions

Only UF Surplus is authorized to officially dispose of university assets. All disposals must adhere to asset management procedures, which strictly prohibit donating property to for-profit organizations.

Special allowances are made for property located outside of Alachua County. Departments should contact Asset Management for more information.

### Submitting a Disposal Request

When an asset is ready for disposal, the department must submit a disposal request within myAssets. UF Surplus offers flexible options: departments can either arrange for UF Surplus to pick up the item or drop it off at the UF Warehouse. Certain items may also require Environmental Health & Safety involvement.

### Disposing of a Non-Mandatory Tracked Asset (Blue/No Decal)

Several options are available:

- **Post Items to the UF Property Swap Listserv:** Open to all faculty and staff; postings should include descriptions and pictures. Acquired property must continue to serve the UF community.
- **Drop Off:** Departments can drop off unneeded property at the UF Surplus warehouse (Building 0811, off Radio Road by Elmore Hall) Monday–Friday from 8:30 am to 3:30 pm.
- **Pick-Up:** UF Surplus will pick up surplus property at no cost. Request pick-ups through your departmental contact. For electronics, UF Surplus provides secure media disposal in compliance with UFIT requirements (sanitized to DoD standards or destroyed).

### Types of Disposal

There are 14 types of disposals, with UF Surplus being the main method. Examples include:

- **Casualty Loss:** Damage from hurricanes, fire, etc.
- **Trade-In:** For another piece of equipment.
- **Return to Vendor:** Receive a refund or credit.
- **Return to Funding Agency:** If associated with an award.

> No asset, regardless of value, may be given or sold to an individual or thrown away without prior approval from Asset Management.

# Lesson 5 of 7 — UF Surplus Property

## Objectives

1. Explain the importance of identifying and tracking property.
2. Outline procedures for managing and tracking property.
3. Determine what surplus property is, locate and use the UF Surplus Warehouse, and describe how users are authorized.
4. List resources available to assist with property management.

> **UF Surplus Property** refers to any university-owned asset that a department no longer needs, whether it is obsolete, damaged, or simply no longer in use.

UF Surplus is located at **976 Elmore Drive, Gainesville, Florida 32611**.

Rather than being thrown away, these items are formally disposed of and transferred to the UF Surplus warehouse. This process supports the university's sustainability goals by giving assets a second life—first offered at no cost to other UF departments, then sold to the public if unclaimed.

## UF Surplus Process

### Step 1: Receive Request

Departments must complete a surplus pickup request for unwanted items, and UF Surplus will coordinate the pickup promptly. Consider listing items on the UF Property Swap ListServ before sending them to UF Surplus. Once items are picked up or dropped off, the property is reviewed for usability. Usable items are then listed on the UF Surplus Online Shopping Portal for two weeks at no cost. Examples include desks, monitors, office chairs, projectors, and conference room tables.

### Step 2: Available to Departments

Unwanted items in working condition are available to other departments at no cost for two weeks after arriving at the warehouse. Electronic items are available after testing by PC/AV Shops, also for two weeks.

Selected items must be picked up within 14 days. UF does not offer delivery, but UF Facilities provides it for a minimal cost.

### Step 3: Auction

If assets are not claimed by departments within the two-week period, they are placed for public auction on the [Public Surplus auction site](https://www.publicsurplus.com/sms/ufl%2Cfl/list/current?orgid=1239) or the [GovDeals auction site](https://www.govdeals.com/en/uf).

## Claiming Surplus Property

There are 3 common ways to claim surplus property:

1. Claim item from the UF Property Swap Listserv (open to all faculty and staff). Send an email to [uf-property-swap-L@lists.ufl.edu](mailto:uf-property-swap-L@lists.ufl.edu) to request to join the listserv.
2. Request a transfer from UF Surplus.
3. [Visit UF Surplus](https://myassets.fa.ufl.edu/ext/%23/whlist). Any property acquired through this website must continue to serve the UF community and is not available for personal or private use.
