## Course Information

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Course Code** | UGO500 |
| **Audience** | UF faculty & staff preparing to acquire a Procurement Card (PCard) |
| **Frequency** | Biennially (every 2 years) |

## Course Description

UGO500 is a mandatory part of the Procurement Card (PCard) application process. This course provides comprehensive guidance on obtaining and properly using a PCard within UF GO, the University's integrated travel and PCard system. It covers essential information, including key resources and best practices, while offering specific examples of PCard misuse and instructions for addressing disputes and fraudulent charges.

Successful completion of the course requires reviewing and acknowledging the Procurement Cardholder Agreement. Additionally, an approved PCard Application is required before a PCard can be issued.

## Course Objectives

- Decide whether or not you need a PCard
- Determine appropriate and inappropriate uses for the PCard
- Explain the process of reconciling PCard charges
- Know the consequences of PCard misuse
- Sign the UF Procurement Cardholder Agreement

**Topics covered:**

- Introduction to the PCard
- Appropriate PCard Use
- PCard Misuse
- Reconciling PCard Charges
- Procurement Cardholder Agreement
- Final Assessment

# Lesson 1 of 6 — Introduction to the PCard

The UF Procurement Card is a credit card with built-in controls to ensure responsible use, as cardholders are spending University funds.

## PCard Uses

- To purchase small-dollar items and services not available in myUF Marketplace
- To pay for frequent (1 or more per year) University business trips

## myUF Marketplace

myUF Marketplace is UF's internal shopping platform and should be your **first choice** for purchasing. If an item is available from a supplier within myUF Marketplace, you are expected to purchase it there rather than using your PCard.

Navigate to myUF Marketplace through [myUFL](https://my.ufl.edu/ps/signon.html). Visit the [myUF Marketplace toolkit](https://hr.ufl.edu/professional-development/toolkits/financial-toolkits/myuf-marketplace) to learn more.

PCards are issued to employees in good standing upon approval from their departmental leadership.

## Types of PCards

There are two primary types of PCards. The type of card determines what can be purchased.

| Card Type | Can Purchase |
|---|---|
| **Commodities** | Supplies, services |
| **Travel & Commodities** | Supplies, services, and allowable travel-related expenses for official UF business trips |

## Obtaining a PCard

### Step 1: Complete This Course

The first step is completing UGO500 to learn about allowable and non-allowable PCard practices and the reconciliation process.

### Step 2: Complete the PCard Application in myUFL

To apply for a PCard, in myUFL navigate to: Main Menu > My Self Service > PCard Application.

Pay attention to:

- Billing address and contact information
- Card type
- **Single Transaction Limit:** the amount any single transaction ("swipe of a card") cannot exceed
- **Cycle Limit:** total amount that can be charged in a 7-day period (Wed–Tues)

### Step 3: Application Approval

After you submit the application, your department's financial approver will need to review and approve it. Once approved, the PCard team will review and process it. You can [check the application status](https://training.hr.ufl.edu/instructionguides/ufgo/ElectronicPCard_CheckCardStatus.pdf) to determine where an application is in the approval workflow.

### Step 4: Activate Your PCard

There are three options to activate your card:

- Visit [Bank of America's Global Card Access site](https://spacardportal.works.com/gar/login), click "Register a Card," and follow the prompts to choose your PIN and set up alerts. Your verification ID is zero followed by your UFID.
- Download the **Global Card Access app**. After activating, you can use the app to see recent transactions, credit limit, available balance, PIN, and to lock/unlock your card.
- **Call 888-233-8855.** You will be prompted to enter:
  - Verification ID – Zero (0) followed by your UFID
  - PCard phone number – 352-392-1331
  - PIN – create your own

## Where Should I Go If I Have Questions?

1. Ask your department's fiscal staff.
2. Consult the [PCard Website](https://procurement.ufl.edu/uf-departments/procurement-cards-pcards/).
3. Use the [PCard Contact Form](https://uf.tfaforms.net/729).

Visit the [UF GO Expense Toolkit](https://hr.ufl.edu/professional-development/toolkits/uf-go-toolkit/expense/) for PCard Application instructions.

# Lesson 2 of 6 — Appropriate PCard Use

PCard purchases fall into three categories: **Allowable**, **Conditional**, and **Non-Allowable**.

## Verify Before Purchasing

Prior to making a PCard purchase, verify the following criteria:

- Appropriate Business Purpose that supports the mission of the department & University
- Unit has available funds to make the purchase
- Purchase meets funding source requirements and adheres to PCard regulations

To learn about funding source requirements, take [PRO302: The Color of Money](https://mytraining-ufshands.sumtotal.host/Core/pillarRedirect?relyingParty=LM&url=core%2Factivitydetails%2FViewActivityDetails%3FActivityId%3D32836%26UserMode%3D0).

## Allowable Purchases

Allowable PCard purchases must have a clear, justifiable Business Purpose that supports the mission of the department and the University. For a non-exhaustive list, see the [Allowable, Conditional, and Non-Allowable page](https://procurement.ufl.edu/uf-departments/procurement-cards-pcards/procedures/allowable/) of the PCard website.

### Business Purpose: Master the 5Ws

A good Business Purpose includes the 5Ws, explaining:

- **Who** — who is benefiting
- **What** — what was purchased
- **When** — the timeframe
- **Where** — the location
- **Why** — the reason/purpose

**Good Business Purpose Example:**
> "Purchased office supplies *(what)* for the Administrative Assistants *(who)* to support daily administrative tasks *(why)* for the next 3 months *(when)* at the Biology Building *(where)*."

## Conditional Purchases

Conditional purchases may be made with a PCard but require [additional approvals or documentation](https://procurement.ufl.edu/uf-departments/procurement-cards-pcards/procedures/allowable/#conditional).

**Common Examples:**

- **Contracts:** If the purchase requires a signed contract (e.g., a three-year license to provide online courses), you must [submit the contract to UF Procurement](https://procurement.ufl.edu/uf-departments/independent-contractors-consultants/) for review and signature before making the purchase.
- **Environmental Health & Safety Items:** Microwaves, freezers, and other equipment/supplies that may pose a risk require [pre-approval from EH&S](https://uflorida.sharepoint.com/sites/ehs-researchservices/LabSafety/SitePages/EquipmentPurchase.aspx).
- **Planning, Design & Construction (PDC) Minor Construction Projects:** All University departments (except IFAS and Housing) must use the [PDC Project Request System](https://pdc.ufl.edu/projects/request-a-project/) to initiate and manage minor construction projects.

### Pre-Approval for Software Purchases

The University has strict [software procurement requirements](https://procurement.ufl.edu/uf-departments/how-to-purchase-software-subscribe-to-software-as-a-service-saas/) to ensure the safety and security of its networks.

Before purchasing software:

1. Verify it is on the list of [pre-assessed software (Fast Path Solutions / FPS)](https://it.ufl.edu/security/audiences/faculty--staff/fast-path-solutions/).
2. If the software is not listed on FPS, submit a [UF IT Help Request Ticket](https://ufl.teamdynamix.com/TDClient/33/IT-Portal/Requests/TicketRequests/NewForm?ID=g6~M3v5svuk_&RequestorType=Service) for an integration risk assessment.

### Gas Purchases

Whether gas can be purchased with a PCard depends on the vehicle type:

| Vehicle Type | PCard Policy |
|---|---|
| **State Vehicle** | Allowable on PCard with vehicle's tag number referenced in documentation |
| **Rental Vehicle** | Allowable on PCard |
| **Personal Vehicle** | Non-Allowable — UF will reimburse you based on the State of Florida mileage rate |

## Non-Allowable Purchases

The PCard **cannot** be used to purchase the items listed below, regardless of funding source. If unsure whether a purchase is allowable, [contact the PCard team](https://uf.tfaforms.net/729) before making the purchase.

### Legal and Regulatory Restrictions

- Any item prohibited by law, regulation, or policy
- Attorney/legal services
- Contributions or gifts
- Guns and ammunition
- Radioactive materials
- DJI Drones ([per UF Research policy](https://research.ufl.edu/compliance/research-security/export-controls/restricted-parties.html))
- Parking citations
- Any purchases from merchants in countries of concern: China (including Hong Kong), Russia, Iran, North Korea, Cuba, Venezuela, and Syria

### Financial and Monetary Transactions

- Capital equipment/asset items costing $5,000 or more
- Cash advances (ATM, Money Order, Wire Transfer)
- Cash credits and refunds
- Gift cards or gift certificates

### University Policies and Specific Agreements

- Coca-Cola products (see University of Florida/Pepsi Pouring Rights memo)
- Household moves (must be processed through UF Procurement Services)
- Insurance (must be processed through UF Risk Management Department)
- Leases (must be processed through UF Procurement Department)

### Personal and Non-Business Use

- Personal convenience items
- Liquor/Beer/Wine
- Meals
- Restaurant purchases

### Split Purchases

Splitting a purchase into multiple transactions to circumvent your single transaction limit is **prohibited**.

### Education and Student Services

- Tuition
- Student fees

### Construction and Renovation

- New construction
- Renovations
- Remodeling/alterations
- Interior design
- Other construction-related services, including window treatments (curtains and blinds), carpeting, painting, window or door replacements, and security systems

Visit [procurement.ufl.edu](http://procurement.ufl.edu/) for a full list of Allowable, Conditional & Non-Allowable PCard purchases.

## Travel

PCards can be used to make travel purchases in alignment with [UF Travel Directives](https://procurement.ufl.edu/uf-go/travel-and-expense/travel-directives/).

A **UF GO Travel Request** should be approved before incurring any travel-related expenses using any payment method, including a PCard.

### Booking Travel

Travel expenses such as airfare, lodging, and car rentals should be booked through the UF GO booking tool (see the [UF GO Toolkit for Travel](https://hr.ufl.edu/professional-development/toolkits/uf-go-toolkit/travel/)) or directly with World Travel Service, the University's travel management company.

### Important Travel Tips

- Your PCard should **never** be added to another user's UF GO profile or other account. If booking travel for another employee or guest, use the "booking for guest" option under your own profile.
- Know your single transaction and cycle limits. Request a temporary limit increase if you anticipate exceeding either limit during your trip.
- When you check in, tell the hotel to separate hotel room charges from personal charges and provide a personal payment method for personal expenses.
- Hotel expenses (e.g., internet access, parking, business phone calls) may only be charged if there is a valid business purpose.
- Take a personal payment method to pay for unallowable expenses or in case something happens to your PCard.

# Lesson 3 of 6 — PCard Misuse

Each PCard holder is responsible for the proper use of their card. Careless or fraudulent use of the PCard has serious consequences.

## What is Considered PCard Misuse?

- Emailing your card number to a merchant or coworker, including sending your PCard number on a purchase order (P.O.)
- Losing receipts or chronically turning receipts in more than 5 days after the purchase date
- Allowing another individual to use your card or card number
- Splitting transactions to circumvent card limits
- Using your PCard for personal (non-business related) purchases
- Repeated non-compliance with processes and procedures
- Failing to have transactions approved within 15 days of the posted date (non-travel) or trip end date (travel)
- Not assigning travel expenses to a report within 15 days of the posted date, or assigning them to a placeholder report

## Personal Use

Your UF PCard should not be used for personal purchases. Your PCard should also not be used with or saved to personal accounts (e.g., Target, Walmart, Best Buy, etc.). If you need to purchase items for UF business from stores where you have a personal account, create a separate UF business account tied to your @ufl.edu email address.

### Reimburse UF

In the event of an inappropriate purchase on a PCard, the cardholder should **reimburse the University within 5 days** of the transaction. The University of Florida may also garnish wages after failure to meet the reimbursement deadline.

## Fraud

Fraud is wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain. There are two kinds of fraudulent PCard transactions:

- **Outside Fraud:** Charges made by someone other than the cardholder (e.g., the card was stolen and used). Contact the bank immediately using the number on the back of your card to report the card as lost or stolen and identify the fraudulent transaction(s).
- **Employee Fraud:** Charges made by a cardholder for their personal gain. If you suspect someone is committing employee fraud, report it immediately to:
  - PCard Team: (352) 392-1331 or [PCard Contact Form](https://uf.tfaforms.net/729)
  - UF Compliance Hotline: 1 (877) 556-5356 or [Report Online](https://www.mycompliancereport.com/report?cid=UOFL)

For information on processing fraudulent transactions in UF GO, see the [Fraudulent Charges section of the PCard Procedures website](https://procurement.ufl.edu/uf-departments/procurement-cards-pcards/procedures/#approvers17).

To learn more about fraud, take [PRO338: UF Fraud Awareness](https://mytraining-ufshands.sumtotal.host/Core/pillarRedirect?relyingParty=LM&url=core%2Factivitydetails%2FViewActivityDetails%3FActivityId%3D43080%26UserMode%3D0).

## Consequences of PCard Misuse

- Employer notification: Dean, Director, Department Head, and/or the Office of Internal Audit notified
- Reimbursement to the University for inappropriate charges or garnishment of wages
- Card suspension
- Card cancellation
- Termination of employment (of the cardholder and/or complicit parties)
- Criminal prosecution

For information on appropriately managing outside activities or interests, visit [UF Conflicts of Interest](https://coi.ufl.edu/).

# Lesson 4 of 6 — Reconciling PCard Charges

All PCard charges should be reconciled timely in UF GO. There are four main steps in the reconciliation process:

1. Collect receipts/supporting documentation
2. Prepare an Expense Report
3. Submit the Expense Report
4. Verify the Expense Report is approved by your financial approver

## Step 1: Collect Supporting Documents

Keep all receipts and any additional forms of supporting documentation for all transactions. Supporting documentation should:

- Be legible
- Be itemized
- Acknowledge receipt of goods or services
- Demonstrate a clear business purpose

Specifically, receipts should include:

- Unit cost of each item/service purchased
- Description of each item/service purchased
- Total cost of purchase
- Vendor name and address
- Date of purchase

**Submit receipts within 5 business days of the transaction.**

- **Option 1:** Upload receipts to UF GO directly, via email, or through the app. See the guide on [Managing Receipts](https://training.hr.ufl.edu/instructionguides/ufgo/2_1_Managing_Receipts.pdf) in the [UF GO Expense Toolkit](https://hr.ufl.edu/professional-development/toolkits/uf-go-toolkit/expense/).
- **Option 2:** Submit receipts to your delegates/approvers.

When the Expense Report is approved, it will create a voucher in myUFL. Once receipts are attached to the voucher in myUFL, you can destroy the physical documents.

## Step 2: Prepare Your Expense Report

- Expense Reports can be prepared by either the cardholder or their delegate.
- All PCard transactions (non-travel & travel) should be reviewed routinely and added to an Expense Report as they post to UF GO. "Routinely" depends on individual card usage — as often as daily but no less than weekly.
- **Non-travel charges** should be added to a PCard Only (Non-Travel) Report. Non-travel charges do not have to be related or share a funding source to be on the same report.
- **Travel charges** should be added to a Travel Report specific to the trip for which the charge was incurred within 15 days of the transaction posted date. Travel charges not added to a trip-specific report within 15 days are considered past due.

When reconciling transactions, you should:

- Verify transactions are valid and appropriate
- Select the appropriate Expense Type
- Update the Chartfield allocation/funding source, as needed
- Upload documentation
- Contact cardholder or stakeholders for additional information as needed
- Add comments

### Missing Receipts

Every attempt should be made to obtain a copy of the receipt or other acceptable supporting documentation. If a receipt cannot be found, the cardholder can create a **Missing Receipt Declaration** in UF GO (see [Missing Receipts guide](https://training.hr.ufl.edu/instructionguides/ufgo/Missing_Receipts.pdf)). This action cannot be delegated. Frequently missing receipts is considered misuse and can result in punitive action.

## Step 3: Submit Your Expense Report

- Only the cardholder can submit a PCard Only Report for the initial submission. If the report is sent back, it can be resubmitted by a delegate after corrective action is taken.
- Reports should not be sent back with the intention of having a delegate resubmit.
- Expense Reports should be submitted with sufficient time to allow for charges to be approved within:
  - **Non-travel charges:** 15 days of the transaction date
  - **Travel charges:** 15 days of the travel end date
- PCard Only reports should be submitted no more than once per week when possible, to allow multiple transactions to be processed on one report (UF is charged per Expense Report).

Visit the [UF GO Expense Toolkit](https://hr.ufl.edu/professional-development/toolkits/uf-go-toolkit/expense/) for step-by-step instructions.

## Step 4: Verify Approval

- Non-travel charges must be approved within **15 days of the transaction date**.
- Travel charges must be approved within **15 days of the travel end date**.
- "Sufficient time" should allow approvers to send reports back for revisions if needed without causing transactions to become past due.
- It is the Financial Approver's responsibility to approve Expense Reports timely. However, cardholders or their delegates should also verify Expense Reports are being approved on time — PCards with past due charges due to approver neglect are still subject to suspension.

> **Remember:** Failing to reconcile transactions in a timely manner is considered misuse and can result in PCard suspension or cancellation.

## Handling Disputes & Fraudulent Charges

### Fraud

**Problem:** I don't recognize these charges.

**Solution:** Contact the bank immediately. If you have issues reaching a representative, notify the PCard Team.

### Disputes

**Problem:** I made the charges, but there's an issue with the purchase.

**Solution:** Contact the merchant directly first to resolve the issue. If they cannot make it right, open a dispute case with the bank.

> Fraud and Dispute claims can only be opened with the bank for **60 days** after the transaction posted date. After 60 days, the charge becomes the responsibility of the cardholder/department.

## UF PCard Program Cardholder Agreement

By signing, the cardholder agrees to the following:

- I understand I am being entrusted with an important financial tool and will use the PCard for authorized business purposes only.
- I will not use the PCard to make personal purchases for others or myself. Personal purchases may result in disciplinary actions up to and including termination of employment and prosecution.
- I am an employee or affiliate in good standing with the University and will follow Florida Law, procurement and travel policies of the University of Florida, and the established guidelines for using the PCard. Failure to do so may result in revocation of card privileges and/or other disciplinary action.
- I have read, understand, and will follow the guidelines in the PCard training. I will retake the training every two years, or as mandated by the PCard Team.
- I will turn in all charge documentation within five days of making a purchase. I understand my transactions should be approved in UF GO within 15 days of the transaction posted date.
- I acknowledge that university policy does not permit me to make or approve purchases from a business entity in which I, my spouse or child have a material financial (more than 5% of stock or assets) or managerial interest (officer, director, partner or proprietor). I will NOT make such purchases with the PCard.
- I have a valid UF email address and agree to read any PCard-related emails I receive.
- I agree that, should I violate the terms of this Agreement, I will be subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment and criminal prosecution. If I make any unauthorized purchases, I will reimburse the University for all charges and costs related to collection. Additionally, any charges I owe the University may be deducted from any money otherwise due to me, including salary or wages, in accordance with University of Florida Rule 6C1-3.042(3), F.A.C.
