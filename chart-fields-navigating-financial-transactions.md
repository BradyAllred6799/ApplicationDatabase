# Lesson 2 of 9 — ChartFields Overview and Accountability

## What is a ChartField?

A ChartField is a single data field in UF's accounting system (myUFL) that tells the story of a financial transaction — where the money came from, what it was for, and where it went — so the University can remain accountable. ChartFields enable control of financial resources, identify and track activity, and drive management and reporting. When combined, they form a **ChartField String** that defines the transaction's "bucket" and supports accurate budgets and available balances.

ChartFields appear across UF financial systems (myUFL, myInvestigator, MyUFMarketplace, myAssets). Using the correct ChartField String at the time of the transaction is essential for clean reconciliations and credible financial reports.

**Example ChartField String:**

| Account | Source | Fund | Dept | Program | Bud Ref | CRIS | PC Bus Unit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 112000 | NA | 211 | 11010000 | 2200 | CRRNT | NA | GRANT |

## Why are ChartFields Important?

ChartFields work across all UF financial systems (myUFL, myInvestigator, MyUFMarketplace, myAssets) to track financial activity using the "buckets" from Color of Money.

To do your job effectively as a fiscal employee, you need to understand how financial transactions at the University of Florida are recorded. This allows you to properly utilize and reconcile these transactions and ensures the integrity of our financial information.

> ChartFields allow the University to track financial transactions so that departments can collect and analyze financial information.

## How do ChartFields Enable Accountability?

ChartFields are the heart of the UF accounting system (myUFL). Each ChartField represents one data field that "tells a story" about where the money came from and where it went, thus enabling accountability. All transactions are entered into myUFL and tracked via ChartFields.

## What Can We Do With ChartFields?

- **Control:** A unique combination of ChartFields allows us to define and control financial resources, creating specific buckets with budget or available balance.
- **Identify and Track:** A ChartField is a data field that stores accounting information. It is used to identify transactions and track financial activity.
- **Manage and Report:** When you combine ChartFields in any type of transaction, the resulting "ChartField String" allows you to manage the funds in your department. Accurate ChartField Strings = Accurate Financial Data.

# Lesson 3 of 9 — Core ChartFields (The Basic Six)

A single ChartField is never enough! The **Basic Six Pack** of ChartFields is required at a minimum to complete the story of a transaction. You will need to enter these fields for every transaction at the University.

| *Unit | Fund | Dept | Account | Program | Bud Ref |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| UFLOR | 102 | 290101100 | 719300 | 4600 | CRRNT |

### The Six Core ChartFields

| ChartField | Description |
|---|---|
| **Business Unit** | Identifies the legal entity; UF uses UFLOR |
| **Fund** | Identifies the source and purpose of the money (the "color of money") |
| **Department ID (DeptID)** | Identifies the department responsible for the transaction |
| **Account** | Classifies the type of revenue or expenditure |
| **Program** | Classifies the functional purpose or activity of the expense |
| **Budget Reference (Bud Ref)** | Identifies the budget year (e.g., CRRNT = current fiscal year) |

# Lesson 4 of 9 — Extended ChartFields and the String

## ChartFields Used for Specific Funds or Colleges

The following ChartFields go beyond the Basic Six and are used for specific funds or colleges.

### Source of Funds (SOF)

Up to 7 characters. Tracks revenue and expenditures from outside sources and identifies who is filling your buckets with money. Required for grant funds or transfers from component units.

Used to track both revenues and expenditures related to an outside funding source such as donors and granting agencies (sponsors). Particularly important for transfers from Component Units such as the UF Foundation and practice plans.

**First letter identifies the source:**

- **F** = UF Foundation, used with Fund 171
- **G** = Granting Agent, used with Funds 201, 209
- **H** = Health Science Center (HSC), used with Fund 171
- **R** = Royalties, used with Fund 213

*Example: F000661 is used for the College of Engineering Dean's Excellence Fund.*

### Project

Up to 8 characters. Identifies activity for projects in contract and grant funds that may extend over years. Allows for segregation and tracking of projects. Primarily required for Sponsored Research and Construction funds.

Enables additional accountability by establishing beginning and end dates, a specific purpose, unique budgeting, and a variety of other measures such as project phases, activities, resource types, categories, and sub-categories.

### Dept Flex

Up to 10 characters. Used by certain departments to further classify a revenue or expenditure at a more granular level. May be used to track faculty annual expense allowances, symposiums, Dean's commitments, conferences, and courses. May be required for certain funds (e.g., Student Financial Aid or Teaching and Technology).

To add a new department flex code, visit the General Accounting website.

### EmplID

8 characters. The employee ID or UFID ChartField is an optional field designed for departmental use to further classify or track transactions. Most often used to track a Principal Investigator or faculty member's expenses.

### CRIS / REEport (IFAS Only)

Up to 6 characters. Only used by IFAS for federal reporting requirements.

## ChartFields Combine to Form a String

A ChartField String can tell you a lot about every transaction at the university prior to seeing a source document. For example:

- Fund 101 + Account 781100 → Capital Purchase of Furniture and Equipment from State Appropriations Funding
- Fund 201 + Account 717000 + Source of Funds G000010 → Lab supplies purchased on a Federal NIH grant

# Lesson 7 of 9 — General Ledger, Reporting, and Reconciliation

## General Ledger

The General Ledger serves three purposes:

1. **Official Accounting Record** — Provides a final and official accounting record for UF.
2. **Aggregate and Summarize** — Aggregates individual transactions and summarizes them by cost center.
3. **Group Financial Transactions** — Groups financial transactions by months and fiscal years to analyze results over a defined time period.

The transactions from every department are consolidated and grouped into the General Ledger. University management relies on it for strategic planning and policy decisions. Annual Financial Statements are also produced using the General Ledger.

## Reporting

The current month's General Ledger is closed no later than the **third business day** of the following month. Delivered Monthly Financial Reports are usually available for review and reconciliation **three business days** after that.

### Accessing Monthly Financial Reports

The only security role required to access Monthly Financial Reports is **UF_ER_User**, which is automatically given to all TEAMS employees. This role:

- Puts "Enterprise Reporting" on your myUFL menu
- Gives you access to Monthly Financial Reports, including:
  - Delivered reports (outside Enterprise Reporting)
  - Prompted reports (inside Enterprise Reporting)

### Delivered Reports

The delivered Monthly Financial Reports (sometimes called department reports or ledgers) are found in myUFL and should be used for reconciliation. Available in both PDF and Excel formats.

**Navigation:** Main Menu > Enterprise Reporting > Department Reports

### Stay Informed

If you are responsible for reconciliations and reviews, subscribe to the **UFACC listserv** to receive alerts when reports are updated each month. Reports are typically available by the **5th business day** of the month. Subscription instructions are on the [General Accounting and Financial Reporting](https://www.fa.ufl.edu/departments/general-accounting-and-financial-reporting/) webpage.

## Reconciliation — What, Why, and Who

The goal of reconciliation is to ensure that the University's transaction data is correct by matching source documents to the Transaction Detail Report for the month.

**Departmental Monthly Reconciliation = Key Internal Control**

### Source Documents

Source documents are receipts and/or evidence of a transaction. Examples include:

- Hardcopies (receipts, invoices, etc.)
- Electronic files (PDF, images, email conversation snips, etc.)
- Images from the Accounts Payable (AP) subsystem (voucher images, etc.)

> Attachments in myUFL are part of the official accounting record. You do not need to print them — you can verify them directly in myUFL.

### Your Role is Important

University accounting is decentralized. Some of the most important delegations of authority are given to transaction preparers, transaction reviewers, and/or approvers. We are all responsible for safeguarding the University through good accounting practices.

Visit the [Department's Contact Page](https://www.fa.ufl.edu/departments/) to reach out to Core Offices for help.

> At the department level, you have an opportunity to be preventative — use the right ChartField String at the time the transaction occurs!
