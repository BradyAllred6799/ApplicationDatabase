### Understanding Spend Authorizations and Expense Reports at UF

**What is a Spend Authorization?**
A Spend Authorization is the formal pre-approval of anticipated travel expenses before costs are incurred. It documents estimated expenses, supports budget and policy review, and routes the request for approval before travel. Spend Authorizations are required before booking travel, making travel-related purchases, or departing on official university business.

**What is an Expense Report?**
An Expense Report records actual business or travel expenses incurred after or during the trip. It is submitted after travel is completed and is used to request reimbursement for approved expenses. Expense Reports are linked to the original Spend Authorization and are a separate step in the travel and reimbursement process.

**Workday vs. Concur**

- **Workday** is the system used for Spend Authorizations, funding, approvals, and later, Expense Reports. It manages the approval workflow and ensures compliance with university policies.
- **Concur** is used only for booking travel arrangements. All approvals and reimbursements are handled through Workday, not Concur.

---

### High-Level Travel and Reimbursement Workflow

**Step 1 – Gather Trip Details**
Collect all relevant information about the trip, including dates, destinations, business purpose, estimated costs, and required documentation. Review the trip for compliance with university policies and funding requirements.

**Step 2 – Create and Submit Spend Authorization**
Enter the trip details into Workday and submit a Spend Authorization on behalf of the traveler. Ensure all required fields are completed and supporting documents are attached.

**Step 3 – Approval Routing**
The Spend Authorization routes through the necessary approval workflow in Workday. Approvers review the request for policy compliance and funding availability.

**Step 4 – Book Travel After Approval**
Once the Spend Authorization is fully approved, travel arrangements can be booked using Concur or other approved methods. No bookings or purchases should be made before approval.

**Step 5 – Submit Expense Report After Travel**
After the trip is complete, submit an Expense Report in Workday to record actual expenses and request reimbursement. Link the Expense Report to the original Spend Authorization for proper tracking.

Following these steps ensures compliance, proper documentation, and timely reimbursement for university-funded travel.

## Lesson 2 of 17: When Pre-Travel Approval Is Required

### Why Pre-Travel Approval Matters at UF

Pre-travel approval is a critical step for any university-funded travel at the University of Florida. It ensures that all travel supports UF's mission, is properly documented, and complies with university policies before any costs are incurred or bookings are made. By requiring approval in advance, UF protects its resources, maintains transparency, and upholds its responsibility to students, faculty, and stakeholders.

### Lesson Summary

- **Understand the necessity of pre-travel approval.** It safeguards resources and ensures compliance.
- **Differentiate between travel categories.** Class A and B require Spend Authorization, while Class C does not.
- **Recognize that comp travel needs approval.** This applies even if a third party pays for it.
- **Confirm travel status and documentation.** This step prevents compliance issues and unreimbursed expenses.
- **Use Workday correctly for travel requests.** Proper use ensures compliance and reduces risks.

---

### Types of Travel and Their Requirements

**Class A Travel**
Class A travel refers to continuous travel of 24 hours or more away from your official headquarters. This type of travel always requires a Spend Authorization before any arrangements are made. Examples include multi-day conferences, research trips, or extended fieldwork. Documentation must clearly state the business purpose and estimated costs.

**Class B Travel**
Class B travel is continuous travel of less than 24 hours that involves an overnight absence from official headquarters. Like Class A, it requires a Spend Authorization before booking or purchasing. Typical scenarios include overnight workshops or meetings that start early the next day. Supporting information should justify the need for overnight travel.

**Class C Travel**
Class C travel is day travel without an overnight stay away from official headquarters. A Spend Authorization is not required for Class C travel, and meals are not reimbursable. Examples include attending a meeting in another city and returning the same day. Only mileage or transportation costs may be eligible for reimbursement.

**Comp / Third-Party-Paid Travel**
Comp travel and third-party-paid travel still require pre-travel approval if the trip is related to UF business. This ensures all official travel is properly documented, even if UF is not directly paying the expenses. Examples include when an outside organization covers conference fees or lodging for a UF employee attending in an official capacity. The Spend Authorization process must still be followed.

> **Remember:** A Spend Authorization is required for official University travel—regardless of who pays—if the trip relates to UF business and meets the criteria for Class A, B, or comp/third-party travel.

---

### Domestic vs. International Travel — High-Level Overview

At UF, domestic travel includes trips within the 50 United States, the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, and the U.S. Virgin Islands. International travel covers all other destinations and comes with additional requirements for approval and documentation. For more details about the international travel processes, see the International Travel section of this course.

---

### Why Confirm Travel Status and Documentation Before Starting in Workday?

- **Policy Compliance** – Ensures only allowable trips are processed, preventing unapproved travel from being entered into the system.
- **Documentation** – Provides justification for the trip's business purpose, aiding reviewers in making informed decisions and ensuring audit compliance.
- **Timing** – Spend Authorization must be approved before booking travel to avoid issues and ensure compliance with UF policy.
- **Risk Reduction** – Early confirmation and documentation prevent delays, denials, and compliance issues, reducing the risk of unreimbursed out-of-pocket expenses.

---

A Spend Authorization is required because this is Class B or Class A travel, depending on duration.

**Case 2 – A Same Day Trip**

**Case 3 – Outside Funding**

## Lesson 3 of 17: Roles and On-Behalf-Of Processing

### Understanding Roles in the Spend Authorization Process

Knowing who does what in the Spend Authorization process is essential for ensuring that travel requests are handled efficiently, accurately, and in compliance with university policy. When you understand the roles and workflow steps, you can support faculty and staff more effectively and help maintain the integrity of UF's travel processes. The "on behalf of" process is especially important for supporting those who need assistance with travel arrangements while upholding all required approvals and documentation.

### Lesson Summary

- **Understand the EDES role.** The EDES ensures accurate and compliant travel requests.
- **Familiarize with the workflow.** Learn the steps from initiation to approval.
- **Ensure accuracy in submissions.** Complete and document all trip details before submission.
- **Recognize role limitations.** Maintain integrity by ensuring no one approves their own transactions.
- **Support faculty and staff.** Proper understanding of roles aids in effective assistance.

---

### Understanding "On Behalf Of" and Key Roles in Workday

**What Does "On Behalf Of" Mean?**
Acting on behalf of another employee means entering, submitting, or managing travel information for someone else, rather than for yourself. At UF, this is typically done by someone with the Expense Data Entry Specialist (EDES) role. This process ensures that employees who need help with travel arrangements can still have their requests handled properly and in compliance with university policy. It matters because the person acting on behalf of another must ensure all information is accurate, complete, and properly documented before submission.

**The EDES Role**
The Expense Data Entry Specialist (EDES) is the primary role for creating and submitting Spend Authorizations on behalf of other employees. The EDES is responsible for entering all required trip details, confirming business purpose, attaching supporting documentation, and submitting the request for approval. Only users with the EDES security role can use the "Create Spend Authorization for Worker" process in Workday. This role was previously known as a Delegate, but now has a formal title and defined responsibilities in the Workday system.

**Employee as Self vs. EDES**
When an employee creates their own Spend Authorization, they use the "Create Spend Authorization" process and submit the request directly. If an EDES creates the request for another employee, the process is started with "Create Spend Authorization for Worker." When the EDES submits on behalf of someone else, the employee traveler becomes the first approver in the workflow before the request moves to the Manager for review.

---

### Workflow Routing in Action

Workday dynamically routes Spend Authorizations based on the roles assigned and the funding sources involved. When one person holds multiple roles, the system automatically skips duplicate approval steps, ensuring that each request is reviewed by the appropriate people without unnecessary repetition.

### Introduction to Spend Authorization Workflows

**Step 1 – Initiate Spend Authorization**
The process begins when either the EDES selects "Create Spend Authorization for Worker" or the employee selects "Create Spend Authorization" in Workday.

**Step 2 – Enter Trip Details**
The EDES or the employee enters all required trip information, including business purpose, dates, estimated costs, worktags, and supporting documentation.

**Step 3 – Submit for Approval**
If the EDES submits, the employee traveler is the first approver. If the employee submits for themselves, the request goes directly to the Manager.

**Step 4 – Manager Review**
The Manager reviews the request for business need, documentation, and policy compliance. The Manager can approve, send back, or add another approver, but cannot edit the request.

**Step 5 – Cost Center Coordinator Review**
After Manager approval, the Cost Center Coordinator reviews the request. This is the only role in the workflow that can edit a submitted Spend Authorization.

**Step 6 – Financial Approval**
Depending on the funding and worktags, the request routes to the appropriate Financial Approver, such as a Cost Center Manager, Grant Manager, Gift Manager, or Project Manager.

**Step 7 – Other Approvers (As Needed)**
Some requests may require additional approvals, such as UAA Spend Compliance, Student Financial Aid, or other specialized roles, depending on the nature of the trip and funding.

This workflow ensures that all necessary reviews and approvals are completed before travel is booked or expenses are incurred.

---

### Roles and Permissions in the Spend Authorization Approval Process

**Expense Data Entry Specialist (EDES)**
The EDES is responsible for initiating, entering, and submitting Spend Authorizations on behalf of other employees. This role ensures all required information—such as business purpose, funding, estimated costs, and supporting documentation—is included before submission. EDES users must have the appropriate security role in Workday to access the "Create Spend Authorization for Worker" process.

**Worker (Employee Traveler or Employee as Self)**
When an EDES submits a Spend Authorization for another employee, the worker (employee traveler) is the first to review and approve the request. This step ensures the traveler confirms the accuracy of the information before it moves forward in the workflow. The worker's approval is required before the request routes to the Manager.

**Manager**
The Manager reviews the Spend Authorization to ensure the trip has a valid business purpose, proper documentation, and aligns with university policies. The Manager can approve, send back for changes, or add another approver as needed. However, the Manager cannot edit the request directly.

**Cost Center Coordinator**
The Cost Center Coordinator is unique in the workflow because they are the only approver who can edit a submitted Spend Authorization. This allows them to correct errors or add missing information before final approval. They review the request after the Manager and ensure that all financial and compliance requirements are met.

**Financial Approver and Other Approvers**
The Financial Approver reviews the funding details of the Spend Authorization based on worktags and the source of funds. This could be a Cost Center Manager, Grant Manager, Gift Manager, or Project Manager, depending on the funding route. Financial Approvers cannot edit the request. In some cases, additional specialized approvers may be required, such as UAA Spend Compliance, Student Financial Aid, or C&G Accountant.

> **Note:** No one can approve their own transaction. If a person is assigned to more than one role in the workflow, Workday will skip duplicate approval steps for that individual.

## Lesson 4 of 17: Beginning the Spend Authorization

### Building a Solid Foundation: Beginning the Spend Authorization

The Spend Authorization form is where every trip begins—and where many errors can be prevented. Completing these fields accurately is essential for ensuring your request is routed correctly, complies with UF policy, and is ready for timely approval.

### Lesson Summary

- **Understand the role of each field in the Spend Authorization Header.** Accurate completion ensures compliance and proper documentation.
- **Complete the Business Purpose and Justification fields.** These are mandatory even without a red asterisk.
- **Use the five Ws in Justification.** Clearly explain the trip's benefits to UF for better approval chances.
- **Double-check all entries.** This is especially important when creating authorizations for others.
- **Ensure thoroughness in filling out all required fields.** This avoids delays and compliance issues.

---

### Options for Starting a Spend Authorization

**Start from the Search Bar**
Use the search bar at the top of the Workday home page to quickly find the Spend Authorization process. Type "Create Spend Authorization" if you are submitting for yourself, or "Create Spend Authorization for Worker" if you are submitting on behalf of another employee. Only users with the EDES role can use the on-behalf-of option.

**Start from the Expenses Hub**
Navigate to the Expenses Hub using the menu from the Workday home page. Once inside the hub, select "Spend Authorizations" from the left panel, then click the "Create Spend Authorization" button.

---

### Spend Authorization Header Fields

**Company**
The Company field should match the traveler's employee profile. For most UF employees, this is *University of Florida*. For Foundation workers, use *UFND*. This field may auto-populate, but it must still be correct.

**Start and End Dates**
The date the travel starts and ends. If the trip includes multiple destinations, the end date should be when the traveler returns to the origin so that the Spend Authorization covers the full trip.

**Description**
Use Description as the title of the Spend Authorization, such as the event or conference name.

**Business Purpose**
Select the Business Purpose from the dropdown list. This field is required even if it does not display a red asterisk. A valid business purpose should align with the reason for the trip, such as *Conference/professional development/workshop*.

**Reimbursement Payment Type**
Use this field to identify how the traveler would be reimbursed for personal expenses. This field may auto-populate, such as *Direct Deposit*.

**Justification**
The Justification field is required even if it does not show a red asterisk. Use the five Ws:

- Who
- What
- Where
- When
- Why

The justification should explain the benefit of the travel to UF and give approvers enough information to understand why the trip is necessary.

**The "For" Field**
When creating a Spend Authorization for another employee, an EDES will have an additional field labeled "For." Use the For field to search by name or UFID. Always double-check the selected employee's details before proceeding.

> **Remember:** Some required fields may not display a red asterisk—always complete Business Purpose and Justification and double-check all entries before moving on.

## Lesson 5 of 17: Add Line Items

### Structuring Your Spend Authorization: The Importance of Line Items

Line items are the backbone of a well-structured Spend Authorization. Each line item specifies your travel details, making it easy for reviewers to understand and approve your request.

### Lesson Summary

- **Line items are crucial.** They ensure clear and compliant travel documentation.
- **Select the correct Expense Item.** This simplifies data entry and reduces errors.
- **Separate line items for destinations.** This ensures accurate tracking and compliance.
- **Use the Memo field wisely.** It helps summarize expenses for quicker approvals.
- **Include accurate amounts and worktags.** This enhances transparency and streamlines the process.

---

### Line Items in a Spend Authorization

**Add**
Select Add to create a new line item. A new line is needed for:

- Each destination
- Each funding source
- Each cash advance request

**Expense Item**
Choose the Expense Item that best matches the travel context. Examples include: comp travel, travel international, travel out of state, travel in state. Selecting different Expense Items can reveal or hide certain Item Details fields. For example, international travel triggers the *Foreign Entities* field, while cash advance requests trigger the *Cash Advance Justification* field.

**Total Amount**
Enter the total estimated amount for that line.

**Budget Date**
Use the trip start date as the Budget Date.

**Memo**
The Memo field is not technically required by the system to submit the Spend Authorization, but it is required in practice. Use it to explain what the traveler expects to spend the funds on and the estimated amount for each expense. The memo amounts should add up to the Total Amount. Example memo content might include airfare, hotel, conference registration, and per diem.

**Cash Advance Requested**
Use Cash Advance Requested only when a cash advance is truly needed. If selected, you must also complete Cash Advance Justification in Item Details and may need a separate line for the cash advance amount.

**Grant Worktag**
If the trip is funded by a Grant, enter the grant worktag in the Grant field rather than using the Designated field.

**Gift Worktag**
If the trip is funded by a Gift, enter the gift worktag in the Gift field rather than using the Designated field.

**Designated Worktag**
If a Designated worktag is used, Workday may automatically populate related fields such as Cost Center, Program, and Additional Worktags. These auto-populated fields can still be reviewed and updated if needed.

**Project Worktag**
Use Project when project funding applies.

**Cost Center Worktag / Program Worktag / Additional Worktags**
Use these as needed based on the funding setup.

**Attendees**
Use Attendees to identify who is traveling on the line, including the employee traveler and any guest travelers. To add a guest, select Guest and enter the guest name.

**Origination and Destination**
Origination is where the traveler departs from. Destination is where the traveler is going. Both fields are required for every line item. For multi-segment trips, create separate lines for each segment.

**Bringing UF Property on the Trip**
Check this field when UF-owned property will travel with the employee, such as a laptop, university-issued device, or lab equipment. For international Spend Authorizations, you may also see instructional text directing you to register the property in myAssets.

**Personal Travel Start and End Dates**
If the trip includes personal travel combined with business travel, enter the personal travel start and end dates here.

> **Note:** Leaving these fields blank when personal travel is involved can cause delays or reimbursement issues.

---

### Lines Tailored to You

**Multiple Destinations**
When a trip includes more than one city or location, add a separate line for each destination.

**Split Funding**
If a trip is funded by more than one source, create a separate line for each funding source with the correct worktags.

**Cash Advance Requests**
Only select Cash Advance Requested if a cash advance is truly needed. The Cash Advance Justification field will appear only if the box is checked and must explain why direct payment methods are not practical.

**Memo Best Practices**
Use the Memo field to list expected expenses such as airfare, hotel, conference registration, and per diem. The amounts in the memo should add up to the Total Amount.

**Foreign Entities**
This field appears for international travel. List all international locations the traveler plans to visit.

**Check for Errors**
Always double-check that all required fields for your scenario are completed.

## Lesson 7 of 17: Personal Travel and Cost Comparisons

### Protecting University Funds: Navigating Personal Travel in Business Trips

When university-funded business trips include personal travel, it's essential to clearly separate the business and personal portions.

### Lesson Summary

- **Separate business and personal travel.** Proper documentation ensures compliance and transparency.
- **Follow the Most Direct Route Rule.** Reimbursements are limited to the most direct business route.
- **Provide a cost comparison for personal travel.** This supports policy compliance and expense justification.
- **Ensure university funds cover only business-related costs.** Justify all expenses in Spend Authorizations.

---

### Personal Travel and Spend Authorizations

**How Personal Travel Changes a Spend Authorization**
When a business trip includes personal travel, you must clearly separate the business and personal portions in the Spend Authorization. This means entering personal travel dates and providing extra documentation to show what the business-only trip would have cost.

**The Most Direct Route Rule**
UF will only reimburse up to the cost of the most direct, business-only route—even if the traveler chooses a longer or multi-destination trip for personal reasons.

**Cost Comparison Requirement**
Whenever personal travel, upgrades, or convenience costs are included in a trip, a cost comparison is required. A valid cost comparison should:

- Represent the cost of the most direct business-only route
- Reflect the trip as though no personal travel were included
- Be created using the booking tool or an email from World Travel Services
- Avoid being limited to a single carrier or a single number of stops

## Lesson 8 of 17: Comp Travel and Third-Party-Paid Travel

### Ensuring Visibility: Why All Official Travel Needs Approval—Even When UF Isn't Paying

All official University travel must be documented and authorized, regardless of who pays for the expenses.

### Lesson Summary

- **Approval is mandatory for all official travel**, even if not funded by UF.
- **Documenting travel ensures compliance** and protects the University.
- **Comp travel is official travel at no cost to UF**, requiring the same oversight as funded travel.
- **Avoid duplicate payments** by not claiming reimbursements from both UF and a third party for the same expense.
- **Accurate entry in Workday is essential**; select the correct expense item and provide supporting documents.

---

### Understanding Comp and Third-Party-Paid Travel

**What Is Comp Travel?**
Comp travel refers to official business travel at no cost to UF. Even when UF is not paying, the trip may still be considered official University travel and must be documented and approved.

**What Is Third-Party-Paid Travel?**
Third-party-paid travel occurs when another organization covers some or all expenses for UF business travel. Pre-travel approval is still required if the travel relates to UF duties.

**Why Approval and Documentation Still Matter**
Approval and documentation are required for all official travel to ensure visibility, compliance, and audit readiness. Not obtaining pre-travel approval or failing to document official travel can result in delayed reimbursements, audit findings, or policy violations.

**Avoid Duplicate Payment**
Expenses paid by a third party cannot also be reimbursed by UF. Receiving payment from both UF and another entity for the same expense constitutes duplicate payment, which is strictly prohibited. If the University pays for an expense that is ultimately covered by another entity, the traveler must reimburse the University.

---

### Entering Comp and Third-Party-Paid Travel in Workday

- Select the correct Expense Item matching the comp or third-party-paid travel context.
- For trips funded by both UF and another party, create separate line items for each funding source.
- Attach supporting documents such as letters or confirmations from third parties.
- Avoid omitting the Spend Authorization, failing to separate funding sources, or requesting reimbursement from both UF and a third party.

---

## Lesson 9 of 17: Cash Advances

### Cash Advances: When and Why They Matter in University Travel

Understanding when and how to request a cash advance is essential for responsible stewardship of university funds. Cash advances are a special exception in the travel process, designed to support travelers only when other payment methods are not practical.

### Lesson Summary

- **Understand the purpose.** Cash advances are for unique travel situations, not general reimbursements.
- **Know the restrictions.** They cannot cover expenses like airfare or costs manageable with direct payment methods.
- **Justify your request.** Provide clear reasons why a cash advance is necessary.
- **Follow the process.** Ensure timely settlement and proper documentation.

---

### Understanding Cash Advances: Rules and Limitations

**What Is a Cash Advance?**
A cash advance is a payment issued to a traveler before a trip to cover anticipated out-of-pocket expenses when direct payment methods are not practical. It is not a general reimbursement tool.

**When Is a Cash Advance NOT Allowed?**
Cash advances cannot be used to reimburse expenses paid personally in advance of a trip, such as airfare. They are also not permitted for any costs that could be paid using the Air Card, P-Card, or other direct payment methods.

**When May a Cash Advance Be Used?**
Cash advances may be appropriate for extended out-of-area stays, field site assignments, remote locations without ATM access, group travel, or remote international locations where direct payment methods are not practical.

**Why Cash Advance Policy Is Strict**
Because cash advances carry higher risk for misuse or error, UF policy limits their use to exceptional cases.

---

### Cash Advances in the Spend Authorization Process

**Requesting a Cash Advance**
To request a cash advance, select the Cash Advance Requested field within the appropriate line of the Spend Authorization. You must also complete the Cash Advance Justification field to explain why direct payment methods are not practical.

**Cash Advance Justification**
Provide a clear and detailed explanation of why alternative direct payment methods are not feasible. Include specific circumstances that necessitate the cash advance and reference relevant policy requirements.

**Timing and Settlement**
Cash advances can be issued up to **30 days** before the start of travel. After the trip, the advance must be settled within **10 workdays**. If the trip is canceled, the cash advance must be refunded immediately.

**Approval Routing**
When a cash advance is requested, the Spend Authorization may require additional approvals. For example, if the trip is funded by 201 or 209 funds, the request will be routed to Contracts & Grants for approval.

**Line Item Setup**
Each cash advance must be entered as a separate line on the Spend Authorization.

**Documentation Requirements**
Submit all necessary supporting documents such as travel itineraries, group rosters, or other relevant materials.

---

### Scenario: Cash Advance for Reimbursement

Dr. Lee asks Jordan about the possibility of getting a cash advance for airfare she already paid for personally. Jordan should not request a cash advance because cash advances are not for airfare and not for reimbursing personal prepayments before travel is complete.

> **Remember:** Cash advances are not for convenience—they are only for situations where direct payment methods aren't practical and must always be justified under UF policy.

## Lesson 12 of 17: International Travel Estimating and Reimbursement Rules

### Getting Estimates Right: The Key to International Travel Compliance

Accurate cost estimation is especially important for international travel at UF. Global trips bring unique rules, risks, and compliance requirements that differ from domestic travel.

### Lesson Summary

- **Understand the M&IE allowance.** Always use the U.S. Department of State rates for meals and incidentals during international travel.
- **Estimate actual lodging costs.** Avoid using a flat per diem.
- **Avoid double-counting costs.** Recognize which incidental expenses are covered under the M&IE allowance.
- **Document your estimates clearly.** Use the Memo field to explain assumptions.

---

### Special Rules for Estimating International Travel Expenses

**M&IE Allowance**
The U.S. Department of State Meals & Incidental Expenses (M&IE) allowance sets the daily rate for meals and incidentals on international trips. Always use these rates for international travel, not domestic per diems.

**Lodging Costs**
For international travel, you estimate and claim the actual cost of lodging, not a flat per diem.

**Included Incidentals**
Some incidental costs are already included in the international allowance and should **not** be reimbursed separately:

- Laundry services
- Maid service
- Portage
- Taxi or rideshare tips

**VAT and Other Expenses**
Value Added Tax (VAT) may be an allowable international travel expense. Only include VAT and other business-related costs not already covered by the M&IE allowance.

---

### Dos and Don'ts of International Travel Estimation

**Building an Estimate**
Start by selecting the correct international Expense Item in Workday. Use the current U.S. Department of State M&IE rates for meals and incidentals, and enter the actual lodging cost for each night of the trip. Only add extra expenses, like VAT, if they are truly business-related and not already covered.

**Memo Best Practices**
Sample Memo: *"Estimate includes airfare, actual lodging, conference registration, and M&IE per State Department rates. Laundry and taxi tips are included in M&IE and not listed separately."*

---

### Why Do International Estimation Rules Differ?

- **Different Allowance Structures** – International M&IE rates bundle meals and incidentals into a single allowance.
- **Risk and Compliance** – Strict rules prevent double-counting and ensure only allowable expenses are reimbursed.
- **Memo as Documentation** – A thorough Memo supports compliance and provides a record for future audits.
- **Error Prevention** – Careful review helps catch mistakes before submission.

## Lesson 16 of 17: After the Trip

### What Happens After the Trip? Understanding the Next Phase of the Travel Process

After a trip is complete, it's essential to understand the difference between pre-travel approval and post-travel actions.

### Lesson Summary

- **Understand the role of Spend Authorizations.** They document estimated costs but do not handle actual expenses.
- **Recognize the importance of Expense Reports.** They require receipts and details of actual costs for reimbursement.
- **Attach all supporting documentation to the Expense Report.** This avoids confusion and compliance risks.
- **Ensure timely submission of Expense Reports.** This prevents delays in reimbursement.

---

### Spend Authorizations vs. Expense Reports: Key Differences

**Pre-Travel Approval (Spend Authorization)**
The Spend Authorization is required before any travel arrangements are made. It documents estimated costs and ensures the trip is approved in advance. Once the trip is complete, it serves as a record of what was planned but does not handle actual expenses or receipts.

**Post-Travel Reimbursement (Expense Report)**
After the trip, an Expense Report must be created to document actual costs. This is where you attach receipts, provide final details, and request reimbursement for eligible expenses. The Expense Report is directly linked to the Spend Authorization but is a separate process.

**Where Documentation Belongs**
Supporting documentation such as receipts, invoices, and proof of payment should be attached to the **Expense Report**, not the Spend Authorization.

**Common Misunderstandings**

- Submitting receipts or final documentation with the Spend Authorization instead of the Expense Report.
- Assuming that approval of the Spend Authorization means reimbursement is automatic.

> **Remember:** A Spend Authorization is the pre-travel approval record based on estimated costs. It does not record final reimbursable expenses after travel—those must be submitted through an Expense Report.

---

### Compliance Risks After the Trip

**Policy Violations**

- Requesting reimbursement for personal or unallowable purchases.
- Submitting travel without sufficient or accurate supporting documentation.

**Consequences**

- Delayed or denied reimbursement
- Need to repay the university for personal or unallowable charges
- Loss of approver or EDES responsibility
- Disciplinary action

**Documentation Pitfalls**
Submitting receipts to the wrong process or failing to reconcile advances can create confusion and compliance risks.

**Timeliness**
Expense Reports and advance settlements must be completed promptly after travel. Delays can result in compliance issues or additional scrutiny.
